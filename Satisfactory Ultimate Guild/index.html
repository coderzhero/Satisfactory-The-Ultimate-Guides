What is Satisfactory?

Satisfactory is a first-person open-world factory building game with a dash of exploration and combat. Play alone or with friends, explore an alien planet, create multi-story factories, and enter conveyor belt heaven!

As a Pioneer working for FICSIT Inc., you're dropped onto the planet MASSAGE-2(AB)b with nothing but a scanner and a dream. Your goal: harvest resources, build massive automated factories, and launch parts via the Space Elevator to complete milestones and progress through tiers (up to Tier 9 in 1.0). Features include co-op multiplayer, vehicle travel (trucks, trains, hypertubes), customization, and optimization challenges.

Satisfactory 1.0 released in September 2024, adding end-game content, performance optimizations, and new features like better multiplayer and UI overhauls.

Getting Started: Beginner Basics

Choose Starting Location

Rocky Desert is ideal for beginners: abundant early resources like Coal, Sulfur, Quartz, Caterium, and water. Fewer aggressive creatures.

Explore First

Scout for pure resource nodes (they sparkle). Prioritize diverse nodes near your Hub.

Unlock the Map Early

Rush Tier 1 Field Research for the map (300 wires, 300 screws, 100 iron plates). Essential for navigation.

Power Management

Start with Biomass Burners fueled by leaves/wood. Unlock Chainsaw and Solid Biofuel ASAP for efficiency.

Progress by completing Space Elevator milestones to unlock new tech. Use the MAM (Molecular Analysis Machine) for research.

Tips & Tricks

Mining
  • Use multiple Portable Miners on one node for faster early extraction.
  • Power miners separately if needed.
Production
  • Connect machines directly with belts: Miner → Smelter → Storage.
  • Plan layouts for expansion—avoid spaghetti bases.
  • Deleting buildings refunds materials!
Efficiency
  • Match belt speeds to production (Mk1: 60/min).
  • Use to-do lists (right-click items).
  • Calculate power/runtime needs.
General
  • Focus on milestones for unlocks like Jump Pads.
  • Set up temporary productions for bottlenecks (e.g., screws).
  • Explore with vehicles later; start on foot.

Basic Factory Builds

Simple early-game setups. Scale up as you unlock tech. Use Satisfactory Calculator for planning.

Starter Iron Plates (30/min)

Pure Iron Node → Full factory.

Mk1 Miner (60 ore/min) ↓ (Mk1 Belt) Smelter (30 ingots/min) ↓ Split to 2x 2x Constructor (Iron Plate: 15/min each) → Merge → Storage

Power: 4-6 Biomass Burners. Later: Coal generators.

Concrete (40/min)

Limestone is everywhere—easy early build.

3x Mk1 Miner (Impure nodes, ~60 total) ↓ Manifold 4x Constructor (Concrete: 15/min each? Wait, actually 40/min total scales) → Storage (use 3-4 constructors for 40+)

Manifold: Belts fill machines evenly.

Screws & Rods (Manifold)

From Iron Ingots.

Ingots → Split Branch1: Constructor (Rods: 40/min) Branch2: 4x Constructor (Screws: 160/min from 40 ingots)

Great for early motors/rotors.

Basic Copper (Wires/Cables)

Pure Copper Node.

Mk1 Miner → Smelter (Ingots) ↓ 2x Constructor (Wire: 30/min each) ↓ 2x Assembler (Cable: 15/min each)
Tip: Build "dirty" early (overclock/underclock later). Use blueprints in-game for replication.
